How we approach siding and gutters

Good siding keeps water out — proper flashing, water barriers, and downspout placement all affect how long the install lasts. We treat siding and gutters as one system, not two add-ons.

Moisture & sheathing

We look for soft spots, failed flashings, and hidden moisture before new siding covers the problem—repairs called out in the scope, not after the fact.

Material selection

Fiber cement, vinyl, or other options matched to architecture, exposure, and how much maintenance you want—honest tradeoffs, not a single default.

Gutters & drainage

Sizing, slope, and downspout routing that reduce overflow, splash-back, and foundation staining—coordinated with roof edges and landscaping.

Trim & transitions

Corners, windows, doors, and rooflines flashed and trimmed so the envelope reads intentional from the curb and up close.

Common projects

  • Full siding replacement: We remove old siding, address any water damage, add proper water barriers, then install new cladding and trim for a clean, finished look.
  • Partial repair & re-side: Targeted rot repair, transitions tied into sound existing siding, color and texture matched where practical.
  • Seamless gutters & downspouts: Runs and outlets planned to move water away from foundations, walks, and basements.
  • Soffit, fascia & color coordination: Trim packages that line up with stone, brick, and rooflines so the refresh looks intentional.

Project snapshot

  • Scope: Fiber cement, vinyl, brick, and seamless gutters
  • Timeline: Varies by scope—quoted per job
  • Prep: Moisture & sheathing check before cover-up
  • Detailing: Flashings, corners & penetrations called out
  • Next step: Free quote & site visit
