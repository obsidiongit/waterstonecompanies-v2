Roofing

A roof isn't just shingles — it's ventilation, flashing, and underlayment working together. We install roof systems with an eye on manufacturer specs and long-term weather performance.

How we approach roofing

Whether you're addressing storm damage or age-related wear, we document scope clearly before work begins—so you know what we're replacing, what we're repairing, and how ventilation and flashing tie into long-term performance.

Assessment & documentation

We evaluate decking, penetrations, and attic ventilation, with photos and written notes when you need them for insurance or records.

Installation specs

Proper underlayment, ice protection, and correct nailing — all installed per manufacturer specs so your roof stays under warranty.

Ventilation & flashing cleanup

Valleys, walls, and penetrations flashed with care; airflow paths reviewed. Magnet sweeps and debris control keep your yard as tidy as we found it.

Options you can compare

Written scope, shingle or metal tradeoffs for your pitch and neighborhood, and photo documentation when insurance or resale needs a clear record.

Common projects

  • Full replacement: Tear-off and re-roof with updated underlayment, edges, and penetrations detailed for your pitch and exposure.
  • Storm damage & leaks: Targeted repairs and documentation when wind or hail drives urgency.
  • Metal vs. shingle: We compare longevity, noise, slope limits, and budget on your specific home—no generic default.
  • Flat & low-slope: Membranes and drainage details for porches, additions, or shallow-pitch planes that asphalt isn't meant to carry alone.

Project snapshot

  • Scope: Metal, shingle, and flat roof systems
  • Timeline: Varies by scope—quoted per job
  • Site prep: Ground & landscape protection discussed up front
  • Documentation: Scope notes & photos when insurance asks
  • Next step: Free quote & site visit
