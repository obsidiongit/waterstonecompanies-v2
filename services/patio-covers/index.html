How we approach patio covers, carports and cabanas

We build patio covers, carports, and cabanas that complement your architecture and stand up to wind and rain—with clear flashing, drainage, and attachment details so the structure behaves like part of the house, not a bolt-on afterthought.

Every site is different: roof tie-ins, post locations, and finish colors all affect how the project looks and performs. We walk through options with you up front, call out what permits or HOA notes may apply, and keep the estimate aligned with the scope so you are not surprised halfway through the build.

Where we focus

Drainage & attachment

Gutters, slope, and ledger or post bases planned so water moves away from the home and connections stay dry and secure.

Structural loads

Posts and beams sized to stand up to local wind and weather — built to last, not just built to look good.

Integration with your home

Roof lines, fascia, siding, and trim choices that read as intentional so the cover looks like it was always part of the design.

Permits, HOA & documentation

We handle setback and height rules upfront so your cover meets local codes — no surprises at inspection.

Common projects

  • Attached solid covers: Patio roofs tied into existing fascia or wall lines with matched pitch and materials.
  • Freestanding carports: Vehicle shade and weather protection when a garage add-on is not the right fit.
  • Poolside cabanas: Semi-enclosed lounge or changing areas that handle splash, sun, and traffic.
  • Lattice & hybrid systems: Mixed shade and open sky for grilling, seating, and seasonal comfort.
